# Training Support Materials

This page supports whoever is running a Giga Meter installation training session — whether a government focal point, regional coordinator, or Giga team member. It covers session preparation, a suggested agenda, talking points, common questions from schools, and post-session follow-up.

For the full installation campaign guide, see the [Government Onboarding Guide](government-onboarding-overview.md).

---

## Before the session — Government checklist

* [ ] Country whitelisted on Giga's backend and schools mapped on Giga Maps
* [ ] Target schools confirmed with school IDs available
* [ ] Installation strategy defined (expert in-person / hybrid / guided remote)
* [ ] Support channel set up (WhatsApp group, hotline, or help desk)
* [ ] Schools notified with session details, pre-session instructions, and the download link
* [ ] Training materials prepared and translated if needed (see materials list below)
* [ ] Device readiness confirmed — schools have identified Windows computers to install on
* [ ] Trainer has walked through the installation themselves at least once

---

## Before the session — What to send schools in advance

Send each participating school the following at least 48 hours before the session:

1. **Session details** — date, time, format (in-person / virtual), and link or location
2. **Pre-session task** — identify two Windows computers that will run Giga Meter. Each should be used daily and connected exclusively to the school's internet
3. **School ID** — confirm the official school ID they will use during registration
4. **Download link** — ask schools to download the installer in advance from [meter.giga.global](https://meter.giga.global/) to save time during the session
5. **Support channel** — share the WhatsApp group link, hotline, or email address schools will use for post-session support

{% hint style="success" %}
Ask schools to join the training session from one of the identified computers so they can install live during the session.
{% endhint %}

## Suggested session agenda

Adapt timing to your audience and format. For a hybrid or remote session with 10–30 schools, allow 90–120 minutes.

| Time | Segment | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | Welcome and context | Explain who Giga is and what Giga Meter measures. Briefly explain how the government will use the data — schools maintain the app better when they understand what it contributes to. |
| 0:10 | What schools need to do | One computer per school, always on, always on the school's internet. Explain that data is collected automatically in the background — there is nothing to do once installed. |
| 0:20 | Live installation walkthrough | Walk through the [Installation Guide](../installation/installation-guide.md) step by step with screens shared. Ask participants to follow along on their own device. |
| 0:50 | Registration walkthrough | Walk through Steps 9–17 (app registration). Emphasise the school ID step — this is the most common point of failure. |
| 1:10 | Show Giga Maps | Open [maps.giga.global](https://maps.giga.global/map) and show how to find a school's data. Highlight that it is publicly accessible. |
| 1:20 | Support and maintenance | Explain the support channel. Emphasise: if a computer is replaced or reformatted, Giga Meter must be reinstalled. Share the focal point registry process. |
| 1:30 | Q&A | See common questions below. |
| 1:45 | Close | Confirm next steps: schools that did not install should attempt within 48h and message the support channel if they hit issues. |

## Key talking points

**Why this matters**
> "This data belongs to your country. Giga Meter creates an independent, verifiable record of what internet schools actually receive — not just what the contract says. Governments use it to hold providers accountable, prioritise upgrades, and report to ministries and donors."

**Why the computer needs to stay on**
> "Giga Meter runs a test automatically — roughly once an hour — while the device is on and connected. If the computer is off, it cannot measure. The more hours a day the device is on, the more data your school contributes."

**Why it must be the school's internet**
> "If the device is connected to a mobile hotspot or a personal router, it will measure that connection — not the school's. The data would not reflect what the school actually receives."

**Why two devices per school**
> "If one computer is switched off, reformatted, or replaced, measurements stop. Two devices give redundancy. We recommend two, ideally one wired via Ethernet."

**What happens to the data**
> "Data is sent securely to Giga's servers and published on Giga Maps. Schools, governments, and the public can see it. No personal data about students or staff is collected — only connectivity measurements."

## Common questions from schools

**"I don't know my school ID."**
Share the school ID reference provided in Step 4 of the Installation Lead guide — what the ID looks like in the country's system and who to contact if it cannot be found.

**"The installer says Windows protected your PC / SmartScreen warning."**
Click **More info**, then **Run anyway**. The app is published by UNICEF and safe to install. See [Installation Guide Step 2](../installation/installation-guide.md).

**"My school isn't appearing in the search."**
Check the school ID format. Try searching by name instead. If the school still doesn't appear, contact the support channel — the school may need to be added to Giga Maps.

**"It says Giga Meter is not available in my country."**
The country may not yet be whitelisted on Giga's backend. Escalate to the government focal point — do not ask the school to continue.

**"The first connectivity test failed."**
This is not unusual — it may mean the internet connection was temporarily slow or unstable at the moment of the test. The app will retry automatically. If tests continue to fail over multiple days, contact the support channel.

**"We only have tablets / Chromebooks."**
Giga Meter currently runs on Windows only. Flag the school to the government focal point — Android support is in development.

**"Do we need to log into Giga Meter every day?"**
No. Once installed and registered, the app runs in the background automatically. There is nothing to do unless the computer is replaced or reformatted, in which case the app must be reinstalled.

**"Can we see our school's data?"**
Yes — open [maps.giga.global](https://maps.giga.global/map), search for the school name or navigate to the country, and click on the school. Data appears within 24–48 hours of the first successful test.

## After the session — Government

* [ ] Confirm which schools successfully installed and registered during the session
* [ ] Follow up within 48h with schools that did not install or had errors
* [ ] Add all school focal points to the support channel
* [ ] Log the session: date, schools attended, schools that successfully installed, open issues
* [ ] Begin monitoring rollout progress — see Installation Lead [Step 8](installation-lead.md)

---

## After the session — Schools

* [ ] Assign one or two staff members as responsible for maintaining Giga Meter
* [ ] Ensure the identified computers remain on and connected during school hours
* [ ] Reinstall Giga Meter whenever a computer is replaced or reformatted
* [ ] Check [Giga Maps](https://maps.giga.global/map) after 48h to confirm data is being transmitted
* [ ] Use the support channel for any issues
