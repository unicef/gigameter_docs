# Installation Strategies

Four approaches have been used across Giga Meter deployments. Most countries use one strategy or a mix of two.

---

## The four strategies

### 1. Expert in-person

Government or country office staff travel to schools and install Giga Meter themselves, providing hands-on support to school IT focal points.

**Country example:** Honduras

**Standard steps:**
- Identify staff who will visit schools and install Giga Meter.
- Develop a deployment plan (route, schedule, equipment).
- Train installation staff (in person or virtually).
- During each installation, collect contact details of the school focal point for maintenance and troubleshooting.
- Set up a help channel for schools to troubleshoot before and after the visit.
- Establish a deployment monitoring plan with the Giga team.
- Follow up with schools that fail to install.

---

### 2. Hybrid — Regional focal points training

School IT staff are trained at regional in-person workshops, then install Giga Meter at their own school.

**Country example:** Sri Lanka

**Standard steps:**
- Adapt and share the Giga Meter installation guide with school principals.
- Ask schools to nominate a focal point responsible for installation and maintenance.
- Organise regional in-person training sessions for focal points; guide them live through the installation.
- Establish a deployment monitoring plan with the Giga team.
- Follow up with schools that fail to install.

---

### 3. Guided remote

School focal points install Giga Meter themselves, supported through virtual training sessions and remote troubleshooting.

**Country examples:** Belize, Albania

**Standard steps:**
- Adapt and share the Giga Meter installation guide with school principals.
- Ask schools to nominate a focal point responsible for installation and maintenance.
- Organise virtual sessions with focal points; guide them live through the installation.
- Establish a help centre (hotline or support desk) for troubleshooting.
- Establish a deployment monitoring plan with the Giga team.
- Follow up with schools that fail to install.

---

### 4. Self-installation (unsupervised remote)

Schools install Giga Meter independently using the installation guide and videos, with optional support via a virtual help desk.

**Country example:** Brazil

**Standard steps:**
- Ensure school contact information is up to date.
- Customise and share the installation guide with schools; request installation within a defined timeline.
- Establish a help centre (hotline or support desk) for troubleshooting.
- Establish a deployment monitoring plan with the Giga team.
- Follow up with schools that fail to install.

---

## Choosing a strategy

| If the country has… | Consider |
|---|---|
| Small number of schools, low school IT capacity | Expert in-person |
| Medium scale, regional structure, moderate school IT capacity | Hybrid (regional focal points) |
| Large scale, decent school IT capacity, strong school principals | Guided remote |
| Very large scale, mature school IT, strong written materials available | Self-installation |
| Heterogeneous mix (urban + remote, varying capacity) | Combine two or more |

---

## Documenting your strategy

Before the rollout begins, document which strategy (or mix) you've chosen and the rationale. This becomes the reference point for the Giga team when troubleshooting and for scaling the rollout later.

The [Installation Strategy Document Template](https://drive.google.com/) covers:
- Chosen strategy and coverage breakdown by school cluster or region
- Rationale (scale, school IT capacity, geography, language, prior experience)
- Adapted steps with country-specific notes
- Support / help-centre arrangement
