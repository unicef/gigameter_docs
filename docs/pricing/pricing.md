### How tiers work

Giga Meter helps governments and partners measure and improve school connectivity. Its application code is open source and free to use. As with any software, there are real costs to host, develop, deploy, operate, and maintain it. Giga Meter is managed on a cost-recovery basis so it can be run sustainably and securely for the schools, governments, and partners that depend on the data, and made available to as many countries and partners as possible.

The Giga Meter application is open source and free to use. Services that cost the initiative money to run, such as hosting, are charged on a cost-recovery basis. The tiers below set out which services are included in each.

Two principles shape the model:

* **No profit is generated.** Any surplus is reinvested in improving the product and reducing fees.
* **Fees fall as the initiative scales.** As adoption grows, the cost of shared infrastructure is spread across more implementations, and the savings are passed on.

We recommend conducting a [total cost of ownership assessment](https://digitalprinciples.org/resource/howto-calculate-total-cost-enterprise-software/) for each implementation. The ongoing costs of support and maintenance are easy to underestimate, particularly for self-hosted deployments, where those responsibilities sit entirely with the implementing organisation. Unsupported implementations tend to produce unreliable data, and unreliable data leads to poor decisions for schools.
